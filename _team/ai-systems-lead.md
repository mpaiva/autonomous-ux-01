The AI Systems Lead is what makes the company *native* to AI rather than just pointed at it. This role builds and maintains the internal agent stack — research, competitive teardowns, first-draft production, accessibility and QA passes, documentation, and the reporting that keeps this very record current.

**Why this role exists now.** Our cost and speed advantage is not luck; it is engineered. Someone has to own the systems that let a small senior team deliver like a much larger one. That engineering is a founding-level concern, not an afterthought.

**What success looks like.** Cycle time and cost structure stay structurally below legacy agencies; the agent stack improves measurably each quarter; humans spend their time on judgment and craft, not toil.
