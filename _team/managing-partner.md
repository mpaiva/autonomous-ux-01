The Managing Partner exists because someone must hold the whole picture: the thesis, the Chairman relationship, and the integrity of the operating record. This role keeps the company pointed at the right problem and makes sure the Chairman can always answer "what was decided, why, what's next, what's at risk" without having to ask.

**Why this role exists now.** A founding team without a single point of strategic accountability drifts. The Managing Partner is that point — not a boss over equals, but the holder of the tiebreak and the Chairman interface.

**What success looks like.** The thesis stays sharp and evidence-backed; the Chairman never feels in the dark; deadlocks resolve quickly with dissent recorded rather than buried.
