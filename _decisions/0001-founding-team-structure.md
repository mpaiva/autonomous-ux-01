## Context

The mandate told us to self-organize rather than adopt borrowed titles. We needed enough roles to cover the company's real early needs — strategy, craft, AI leverage, demand — without inventing a bloated org chart for a company with no customers yet.

## Decision

Organize as **four founding functions**, each justified by a concrete need:

1. **Managing Partner** — thesis, Chairman reporting, decision tiebreak.
2. **Design Principal** — craft, delivery quality, the AI-UX pattern library.
3. **AI Systems Lead** — the internal agent stack that makes us AI-native.
4. **Growth Lead** — market, pricing, demand, client relationships.

**Decision rule:** in-domain calls belong to the owning lead; cross-domain calls need any 2 of 4; deadlocks go to the Managing Partner with dissent logged; legal/financial/ownership/ethical decisions escalate to the Chairman.

Deferred roles (Finance/Ops, Recruiting, additional designers) are added when revenue justifies them — see [scaling decision]({{ '/decisions/0008-ai-native-operating-model/' | relative_url }}).

## Rationale

Four functions map exactly to the four things that determine survival: are we solving the right problem (Managing Partner), can we do the work well (Design Principal), can we do it faster/cheaper than rivals (AI Systems Lead), and will anyone pay (Growth Lead). Fewer would leave a survival-critical area unowned; more would be theater.

## Assumptions & risks

- **Assumption:** a single operator (plus the agent fleet) can hold multiple functions early; these are *roles*, not yet four separate hires.
- The Managing-Partner tiebreak concentrates some power — mitigated by mandatory dissent logging.
