## Context

"AI-native" has to mean something operationally, not just in marketing. We defined how AI actually changes how the company runs and scales.

## Decision

Agents are first-class operators for **research, competitive teardowns, first-draft IA/flows/copy, accessibility & QA passes, documentation, and Chairman reporting.** Humans own **taste, judgment, client relationships, and the final craft.**

**Scaling principle:** productize delivery into repeatable playbooks so each designer is force-multiplied by agents; knowledge compounds in the shared pattern library. Add headcount (additional designers, then Finance/Ops, then Recruiting) only when revenue justifies it.

**Individual growth:** a dual track (craft vs leadership); "agent leverage" — the ability to get more and better output through the agent stack — is a core reviewed competency for everyone.

## Rationale

This is the structural moat: a lower cost base and faster cycles than legacy agencies, while staying more senior than the freelancer market. It is also dogfooding — we can't credibly sell AI-product design if we don't operate AI-natively ourselves. The two research briefs and this operating record were produced this way.

## Assumptions & risks

- **Assumption:** AI-augmented work is *perceived* as high quality, not cut-rate. Tracked as [R-03]({{ '/risks/03-quality-perception/' | relative_url }}).
- **Assumption:** the human-owned final 20% is genuinely what clients pay for, protecting margins.
