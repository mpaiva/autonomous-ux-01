## Context

"AI-native design company serving AI startups" can mean two very different things: (a) a generalist studio that happens to use AI tools internally, or (b) a studio specialized in the UX problems *unique to AI products*. We had to choose what we're known for.

## Decision

Be **both AI-native in how we operate and AI-specialized in what we're expert at.** Our craft specialty is the UX of AI products — agent supervision, trust/explainability, non-deterministic output, onboarding for AI — and our operating model uses agents internally to deliver it faster and cheaper.

## Rationale

The generalist design-subscription market is crowded and commoditized (DesignJoy, Superside, et al. compete on throughput and price — see [Research]({{ '/research/0001-design-studio-pricing/' | relative_url }})). Specialization in AI-product UX is where the pain is sharpest and the competition thinnest. Operating AI-natively gives us a cost/speed edge *on top of* the specialization, rather than as our only claim.

## Consequences

- Marketing, IP, hiring, and the pattern library all orient around AI-product UX.
- We say no to generic marketing-site/graphic-design subscription work.

## Assumptions & risks

- **Assumption:** the AI-product-UX niche is large and durable enough to sustain a studio — supported by funding levels and pain evidence, but not yet validated with our own paying clients. Tracked as [R-01 demand risk]({{ '/risks/01-demand/' | relative_url }}) and [R-02 differentiation risk]({{ '/risks/02-differentiation/' | relative_url }}).
