## Context

An unknown studio in a niche needs authority before it can sell. We chose how we earn attention and turn it into pipeline.

## Decision

A four-part go-to-market:

1. **IP / content-led inbound** — publish the open AI-UX Pattern Library and public teardowns of real AI products' UX. Earn authority in the pattern vacuum.
2. **Paid audit wedge** — convert interest into a small, fixed-fee first transaction.
3. **Founder-led outbound** — targeted outreach to seed–Series A AI startups via investor and operator networks.
4. **Build in public** — this operating record is itself a GTM asset: we run our company the way we'd help a client run theirs.

## Rationale

Content/IP is the cheapest credible authority play for a specialist, and it compounds. The audit lowers the activation barrier identified in research (founders defer design until forced). Outbound through investor networks reaches exactly the funded seed-stage ICP. Building in public demonstrates our competence as the proof itself.

## Assumptions & risks

- **Assumption:** teardowns and a public library generate qualified inbound rather than just applause. Tracked alongside [R-01](/risks/01-demand/).
- **Assumption:** building in public doesn't leak so much strategy that it aids competitors — see [R-06](/risks/06-confidentiality/).
