## Context

We had to choose a business model: classic hourly project agency, unlimited design subscription, fractional lead, or something layered. The model shapes cash flow, sales motion, and how we scale.

## Decision

A **layered productized partnership** with a deliberate path from low commitment to high:

- **Wedge — AI UX Audit:** fixed-fee, 1–2 week teardown of an AI product's trust/agent/onboarding UX + prioritized roadmap. Low risk for the client, high signal for us, natural lead-in to a retainer.
- **Core — Embedded AI design partner:** monthly subscription/retainer; an agent-augmented fractional design team.
- **Build — AI UX systems:** project-priced design systems, pattern libraries, and agent-supervision / eval / onboarding builds.
- **IP — the open AI-UX Pattern Library:** free, public, a lead-generation and credibility engine, not sold directly.

## Rationale

The fixed-fee audit de-risks the first transaction and qualifies the client, solving the "founders under-invest until forced" problem by giving them a small, concrete first step. The retainer is where the durable revenue and compounding relationship live. Open IP is the cheapest credible way for an unknown studio to earn authority in a pattern vacuum.

## Assumptions & risks

- **Assumption:** audits convert to retainers at a workable rate — unvalidated; the central GTM experiment. See [R-01](/risks/01-demand/).
- **Assumption:** open-sourcing patterns helps more (authority, inbound) than it hurts (competitors copy). Accepted deliberately.
