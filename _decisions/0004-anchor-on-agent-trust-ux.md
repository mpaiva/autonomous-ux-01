## Context

Within AI-product UX, we needed to name the *specific* problems we lead on — the ones worth building a company around — rather than claiming all of AI design.

## Decision

Anchor on the three best-evidenced, most pay-worthy problems:

1. **Agent supervision & human-in-the-loop UIs** — overview panels, oversight flows, activity logs, work reports. The most concrete, least-solved cluster; a genuine pattern vacuum.
2. **Trust & explainability that unblocks enterprise pilots** — the named #1 killer of early enterprise pilots.
3. **AI onboarding that converts trials and cuts churn** — a recurring, ROI-clear trigger for spend.

## Rationale

This is the decision where our two research streams *converged*: the most painful, least-solved problem (agent/trust UX) is the *same place* as the competitive white-space (no named studio owns eval/observability and agent-interaction UX). Owning the hardest problem with no incumbent is the strongest position available to us. ([pain research](/research/0002-ai-startup-design-pain/), [pricing/competitor research](/research/0001-design-studio-pricing/))

## Consequences

- The AI-UX pattern library leads with agent-supervision and trust patterns.
- The AI UX Audit (our wedge) is scoped around exactly these three problems.

## Assumptions & risks

- **Assumption:** the pattern vacuum persists long enough for us to plant a flag; incumbents (Adam Fard, 925Studios, Punchcut, Clay, Lazarev) could move in. Tracked as [R-02](/risks/02-differentiation/).
