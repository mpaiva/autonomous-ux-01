## The problem we're built around

AI products are non-deterministic. The same input can produce different output, so errors are not edge cases to be patched — they are the normal operating condition, and they have to be *designed for*. Two consequences fall out of this, and both are now blocking real revenue for AI startups:

1. **Trust is the enterprise pilot-killer.** Market research is blunt about it: the biggest killer of early enterprise pilots is not the model failing — it's the lack of organizational trust in a black-box system. Buyers won't integrate a tool they can't explain, audit, or supervise. ([see Research](/research/))
2. **The hardest UX problems have no established patterns yet.** Agent supervision and human-in-the-loop interfaces — overview panels, oversight flows, activity logs, work reports, trust calibration — are the most concrete, least-solved cluster in the field. There is a genuine *pattern vacuum*.

Founders treat design as polish applied after engineering, so they under-invest until something forces the issue: a stalled enterprise pilot, a fundraise where "80% accuracy" reads to investors as "20% failure," or onboarding friction that quietly caps retention.

## Why now, and why us

Our two research streams converged on the same point from opposite directions:

- The **most painful, least-solved problem** for AI startups is agent-supervision / trust UX.
- The **competitive white-space** — where no named studio has planted a flag — is eval/observability and agent-interaction UX.

The most painful problem and the open market position are the *same place*. That is the wedge.

## Who we serve first

> **Seed to Series A applied-AI startups** (≈10–50 people) shipping agentic or LLM products to real users — past the prototype, now hitting the trust wall that blocks retention and enterprise deals.

We deliberately do **not** start with pre-seed (no budget, DIY design) or large AI labs (in-house design teams). Seed-stage AI startups are well-funded and need to convert raw capability into a fundable, demoable, trustworthy product — that's exactly the work we're best at.

## The moat: AI-native by construction

We don't just *design for* AI — we *operate as* an AI-native company. Agents do the research, competitive teardowns, first-draft IA and flows, accessibility and QA passes, documentation, and Chairman reporting. Humans own taste, judgment, client relationships, and final craft. The result is a structurally lower cost base and faster cycle time than legacy agencies, while staying more senior than the freelancer market.

This operating record itself — the site you're reading — is part of the moat and part of the go-to-market: we build our company in the open, the way we'd help a client build theirs.
