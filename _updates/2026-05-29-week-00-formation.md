Chairman — Throughline exists on paper, and its operating record is the site you're reading. Here is the first week in one screen.

## What we decided

We made **ten founding decisions** ([all here]({{ '/decisions/' | relative_url }})). The spine of them:

- **What we are:** an AI-native studio specialized in the UX of AI products — not a generalist agency that happens to use AI tools.
- **Who we serve:** seed–Series A applied-AI startups (~10–50 people) hitting the trust/usability wall.
- **The wedge:** agent-supervision, trust/explainability, and AI-onboarding UX — the most painful, least-solved problems.
- **How we sell:** a fixed-fee AI UX Audit → embedded retainer → systems builds, with an open AI-UX Pattern Library as the inbound engine.
- **How we operate:** AI-natively — agents do the leverage work, humans own taste, trust, and final craft.

## Why it's defensible

Our two research streams converged: the **most painful problem** for AI startups (agent/trust UX) is the **same place** as the **competitive white-space** (no named studio owns eval/agent-supervision UX). We're aiming the company at the hardest problem with no incumbent. Both briefs are cited in [Research]({{ '/research/' | relative_url }}).

## What we learned

- Productized design subscriptions sit at ~$5–8k/mo; fractional design ~$3–10k/mo — we can price a *specialized* AI design partner above that on scarcity ($10–18k/mo hypothesis).
- The thinnest evidence is **willingness-to-pay** — so demand is our #1 tracked risk, and the audit wedge is our cheap way to test it.

## What's at risk

Six risks tracked; the one that can kill us is **demand** ([R-01]({{ '/risks/01-demand/' | relative_url }})). Everything is sequenced to validate that first.
