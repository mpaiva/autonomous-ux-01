> **Resolved 2026-05-29 ([P-0002]({{ '/log/0002-make-public-and-deploy/' | relative_url }})):** The Chairman ratified the **public** posture and authorized deployment under the `mpaiva` account. This risk is now **accepted** — build-in-public is the deliberate default. The mitigation discipline below still applies: keep live pricing experiments and any client-identifying detail out of the public record.

## The risk

This operating record is a candid, traceable account of strategy, pricing, and risks. Published publicly (and indexed by search engines), it hands competitors a clear view of our thinking — and pricing experiments become visible to prospects mid-negotiation.

## Why it's still open

Publishing has not happened. **Deployment is a Chairman-authorized handoff**, not something the team does silently — it requires the Chairman's GitHub account and exposes internal strategy. See [D-0009]({{ '/decisions/0009-operating-record-and-hosting/' | relative_url }}).

## Mitigation options (for the Chairman to choose)

- **Public** — full build-in-public benefit, full exposure. (Default GTM stance, pending sign-off.)
- **Private repo / access-gated** — keep the operating record internal; publish only curated content (teardowns, pattern library) for GTM.
- **Hybrid** — public marketing + pattern library; private decisions/pricing/risks.

## Open questions

- Does the Chairman want the operating record public, private, or hybrid?
- Which artifacts are GTM assets vs. internal-only?
