## The risk

The single risk that can kill the company. Our thesis assumes seed–Series A AI startups will pay outside money for design help. Willingness-to-pay evidence is genuinely thin (see [Research]({{ '/research/0002-ai-startup-design-pain/' | relative_url }})) — much of the "budget" data circulating is for marketing retainers, not design.

## Why it matters

Everything else — positioning, pricing, hiring — is downstream of someone signing a check. If demand is weak or the buyer defers indefinitely, no amount of craft saves us.

## Mitigation

- The **fixed-fee AI UX Audit** is explicitly designed as a low-barrier first transaction to test willingness-to-pay fast and cheap.
- **Validation gate:** 5 target-ICP founder conversations + at least 1–2 sold audits before scaling spend on the model.
- Keep the cost base low (AI-native ops) so the runway to find product-market fit is long.

## Open questions

- What budget line does an AI UX Audit come out of at a seed-stage startup?
- Does the audit reliably convert to a retainer, or stall as a one-off?
